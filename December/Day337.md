Now, the thing about these mysteries is that they are not, as some might suppose, mere events locked in the past. They are rather like doors—or better, like the dawn which, once having broken, continues to light every successive day. The first door is the Resurrection, where Death itself was undone and a new kind of life, solid and everlasting, sprang from the tomb. The second is the Ascension, where our Lord, having completed His work, was taken up, His hands lifted in a blessing that has not ceased. Then comes the rushing wind and the flame of Pentecost, where the very breath of God filled those waiting hearts with a courage not their own. Following this is the Assumption of Our Lady, a sure sign that what was promised to the Head is also promised to the members, her bodily glory a quiet herald of our own. And finally, the Coronation, where she who said ‘yes’ is crowned, showing us that the humble shall indeed be exalted and that all faithful service ends in a kingdom.

These truths have been given shape and colour by the hands of craftsmen. We are shown Piero della Francesca’s _Resurrection_, with its serene Victor stepping from the sarcophagus at dawn. We see Memling’s _Ascension of Christ_, where He rises in light from the rocky earth. El Greco presents us with his _Descent of the Holy Spirit_, all vibrant flame and elongation. Titian’s great vision of the _Assumption of the Virgin_ has her borne aloft by angels, and Velázquez, with his profound naturalism, shows us _The Coronation of the Virgin_ by the Father and the Son.

The meditation proper is to take these pictures not as ends, but as windows. Look through them to the reality: see your own death and resurrection in the first, feel the perpetual blessing of the second, hand your fears to the divine wind of the third, trust in the faithfulness shown in the fourth, and let your heart yearn for that heavenly country where a crown awaits. For the promise woven through these five glorious things is nothing less than our own share in that eternal life, the world made new, and a welcome home.
